Program Overview

The U.S. manufacturing and service economy faces acute workforce shortages, rising demand, and safety/sustainability pressures, elevating intralogistics as a national competitiveness priority in the post pandemic era. Human-centered collaborative automation addresses these pressures by retaining people for complex, judgment-heavy work while delegating repetitive intralogistics operations to autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) that sense, avoid obstacles, and navigate safely around humans.

This International Research Experiences for Students (IRES) program contributes to enabling next-generation, human-centered collaborative automation leading to more responsive, resilient and efficient work environments across numerous critical sectors such as semiconductors, intralogistics operations, healthcare, and retail.
