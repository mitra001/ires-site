Program Overview

The global shift toward smart manufacturing and advanced service systems has increased the need for supply chains that are resilient, efficient, and responsive. Meeting this need requires collaborative ecosystems that integrate humans and autonomous mobile robots (AMRs). These systems support critical sectors such as semiconductor production, healthcare, retail, and e commerce. This NSF-supported IRES program prepares students to contribute to this transformation through international research and cross-disciplinary training.

The core scientific theme focuses on Collaborative Human-Mobile Robot Operations (CHROps). Students will examine optimization-based decision models, artificial intelligence-based methods, data-driven tools for human-robot interaction, and strategies that support safe and efficient workflows in modern supply chain and service environments.

The program offers a fully-funded 10-week experience. Participants receive a stipend of $700/week for 10 weeks. In addition, travel, housing, and food during the Germany stay are also covered. This support ensures that students can focus on research, professional development, and cultural exchange without financial barriers.

Research Focus Area

Student projects will explore use-inspired problems in collaborative human-mobile robot operations, including:

  • Path planning and navigation for mobile robots in human-occupied environments
  • Fleet coordination and resource allocation for AMR systems
  • Data-driven decision-making for human-robot interaction
  • Task assignment and scheduling in mixed human-robot workflows
  • Optimization of facility layout and material flow
  • Human-centered automation across manufacturing and service sectors

Benefits to Participants

Participants will gain:

  • A fully funded international research experience supported by NSF
  • International research experience at a leading German institution
  • Technical training across operations research, machine learning, robotics, and automation
  • Hands-on exposure to real problems faced by modern supply chain systems
  • Professional mentoring from U.S. and German faculty teams
  • Strengthened preparation for graduate study and advanced careers
  • Cultural immersion that supports global awareness and interdisciplinary collaboration
